About

The idea behind LunaVerse

A personal project built from scattered notebooks, half-finished flashcard apps, and the slow realization that learning sticks best when it feels like exploring.

Why LunaVerse exists

LunaVerse started as a single page listing German verbs I kept forgetting. It grew into a full platform once I realized the same problem — scattered notes, no single place to return to — applied to Chinese characters I was relearning and English idioms I'd never organized.

The name comes from a habit: most studying happens at night, when the apartment is quiet and the moon is the only other thing awake.

What you'll find here

Structured lessons across English, Chinese, and German, organized by real proficiency levels — not arbitrary difficulty labels. Original short stories written specifically for language learners, at a range of levels. A personal blog documenting what's actually working, and what isn't. And five small games designed to make vocabulary review feel less like a chore.

How it's built

LunaVerse is intentionally simple under the hood: plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with no frameworks and no build step, so it can run directly on GitHub Pages without any external dependencies. The content lives in readable JSON files, which keeps it easy to extend with new lessons and stories over time.
